[ 02 ] HOW A DG SET WORKS

The science of standby power.

A diesel generator quietly converts fuel into electricity — and steps in the moment your mains supply fails.

Diesel engine — the heart

A diesel engine compresses air to high pressure, then injects diesel which auto-ignites. This combustion spins the crankshaft at 1500 RPM — the standard speed for 50 Hz Indian power.

Alternator — the conversion

The spinning crankshaft turns the alternator's rotor inside a magnetic field. This induces an alternating current — your raw electricity output.

AVR — clean, stable voltage

The Automatic Voltage Regulator keeps output steady regardless of load. Sensitive equipment — servers, MRI machines, CNC lathes — needs this stability.

ATS & control panel — the brain

An Automatic Transfer Switch senses mains failure and starts the genset within 10 seconds. The digital control panel monitors voltage, frequency, oil pressure and fuel level in real-time.

Silent canopy — the polite shell

An acoustic enclosure with absorbent foam, residential silencer and vibration mounts brings noise down to ~75 dB — quiet enough for hospitals, schools and apartment basements per CPCB norms.
